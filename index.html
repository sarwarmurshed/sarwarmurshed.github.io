Research

My research focuses broadly on developing algorithms for efficient and scalable machine learning systems. I am particularly interested in hardware-friendly deep learning algorithms and its applications in biometrics, vision, language, and speech using the edge computing paradigm. I am passionate about applying my research to accelerate progress in science and engineering. My research is supported by CITeR, Verizon and Badger Technologies. Before joining UW-Green Bay as a tenure-track assistant professor, I was a research assistant at Clarkson University, where I earned my Ph.D. in the ECE department. Additionally, I had the privilege of working on several exciting industrial projects throughout my career. Notably, I spent nearly five years at Samsung R&D, where I had the opportunity to work on IoT, SHealth and Tizen project.
